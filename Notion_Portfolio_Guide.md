# Build a Professional Portfolio in Notion from Scratch (Step-by-Step Guide)







Imagine walking down a busy street filled with stores. One store has big, clean windows showing off beautiful products with price tags, neat labels, and a welcoming layout. Another store is closed with dusty windows and no sign of what’s inside.

*Which one would you walk into?*

The answer is obvious, right?

That’s exactly what a portfolio does for you. It’s your **“professional shop window”**. It shows potential employers what you can do, how you think, and what you’ve built at a glance. So, whether you are a *freelancer, a job seeker, or a professional*, creating a portfolio that truly represents your skills and experiences can make all the difference.

In this guide, I’ll walk you through how to build a stunning and organized portfolio in **Notion** that you can easily customize as you grow. If you prefer to follow along visually, I’ve also created a YouTube video tutorial that covers everything you need to know.

### Why Use Notion for your Portfolio?
- **Free to use:** Notion offers a free plan, which means that you can create and share your entire portfolio without spending a dime.
- **Easy to Update:** You can quickly make any changes or add new content without having to go through a complicated process.
- **No-Coding Required:** You can build and customize your portfolio without any coding Knowledge.
- **Customizable Templates:** If you don’t want to create a portfolio from scratch, you can select from a variety of portfolio templates in Notion and tailor them to fit your needs.
- **Visually engaging and interactive:** Notion allows you to embed multimedia elements like videos, images, audio, and others, which makes your portfolio more engaging and interactive.
- **Easy to Share:** You can easily share your Notion portfolio with others with just a few clicks.
### What to Include in Your Portfolio
There are important sections that your portfolio should have. These include;

- About Me section
- Skills section
- Projects section
- Contact Information section
  
Let’s briefly talk about each section

1. **About Me Section:** This section is your chance to introduce yourself. Think of it like an elevator pitch. If you have just one minute in an elevator to tell someone about your services, what would you do? You’d need to be brief, but then, your words should be strong enough to leave an impact, such that they would feel the need to reach out to you. In the same way, your ‘About Me’ section should be brief yet captivating. It should contain these key elements;
- Who you are
- What you do and whom you do it for
- Your unique skills
- Your Passion
  
2. **Skills Section:** This is the section where you highlight what you are great at. So, ask yourself: *“What value do I bring to the table?”* After reflecting on your strengths, list them clearly — make sure you include your soft skills as well as your technical skills, because they are both equally important in showing your full range of capabilities.

3. **Projects Section:** This is the section where you showcase your **best** work. Notice that the emphasis is on ‘best’ because this is the section that would give your potential employers confidence in your abilities, so you need to make sure that you’re showcasing your most impactful work. Another thing that is worth noting is that they may not have the time to go through all your work, so even though it’s just three of them you’ve got, ensure that they are the ones that showcase your skills the most and match the role you’re applying for.

4. **Contact Information Section:** This is where you’d add all the information that will help your potential employers reach you. Having built a well-structured and captivating portfolio, the last thing you want is to have them struggle to reach you. Mind you, they may have to go through many portfolios, so if they are finding it hard to reach you, that could cost you opportunities. By the end of this article, you will learn how to create this section and how to make it as seamless as possible.

### Step-by-Step Guide to Creating a Portfolio in Notion
**STEP 1: Create a new page**

a. In the left sidebar, click on “*Create a new page.*”

[Image by Author](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:1400/format:webp/1*6i6BKoGVYWFvPoy5XBKk6A.png)

b. Give your page a title, e.g., Portfolio

**STEP 2: Add a Cover Image for your page**

a. Hover on the title and click on “*Add cover.*”

b. Click on “*change cover*” to change the default cover image to your preferred one. Notion gives you four easy options to add a cover image:

- Choose from Notion’s built-in gallery
- Upload an image from your device
- Paste an image link
- Select from Unsplash’s free image library
  
[Image by Author](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:1400/format:webp/1*ZrULJbjtqQ68Mt7PoCaKFQ.png)

c. If you need to adjust the position of the cover image, click on “*Reposition*”, hold down your mouse, then drag it around until it fits perfectly. Once you’re satisfied with the replacement, click on “*Save position*”.

**STEP 3: Create the About Me section**

a. Click the **“ + ” icon**

[Image by Author](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:1400/format:webp/1*4BOetZiEcy2Id_x88QdbQQ.png)

b. Select Heading from the dropdown

[Image by Author](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:1400/format:webp/1*VDdQgOyGMkuE8h1FcTg6xQ.png)

c. Type **“About Me”**

d. Add a short description about yourself right below the heading

[Image by Author](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:1400/format:webp/1*fmmpeQG9l_2J7tktosHyGQ.png)

STEP 4: Create the Skills Section
a. Click the **“ + ” icon**

b. Select Heading from the dropdown

c. Type **“Skills”**

d. List your soft and technical skills below the heading. To do this,

- Click the **“+” icon**
- In the dropdown, scroll down to find and select the type of list you want, e.g., a bulleted list.
- Alternatively, in the dropdown search bar, type ‘list” and choose your preferred list style from the results.
  
[Type “list” in the dropdown search bar and choose your preferred list style](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:1400/format:webp/1*V3Hbk2uLZWELKqM07_8G9Q.png)


**STEP 5: Create the Projects Section**

Remember that this is where you’ll showcase your work, so instead of listing your projects, it is best practice to use **Gallery view** — this layout makes it visually appealing and organized. It also allows you to showcase multiple projects at a glance and create project cards with titles, images, and a short description. This makes it easy for viewers to click and quickly scan through each project.

[Image by Author](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:1400/format:webp/1*inZGCvEAlKJYwuNwoRxilw.png)

To create this section,

a. Click the **“+” icon** or press the forward slash key (/) on your keyboard

b. Type **“Gallery view”** and select it

c. Give it a title, e.g., Work Samples


d. Click on **“+ New page”** to start creating your first project card

e. Within the new page, give the project a title

f. Add a **“Project Description”** Section

- Click the **“+” icon** and select **Heading**, or type “/heading”
- Type **“Project Description”**
- Just below, briefly describe the project — what it’s about, your role, and what you were trying to achieve.
  
g. Add a Divider

Type “/divider” or click the **“+” icon** and select **Divider**. This helps you to separate each section and keeps the page tidy
h. Add a **“Techniques Used”** Section

- Again, add a heading and label it **“Techniques Used.”**
- List out the skills, tools, or technologies you used to complete the project. For example:
*HTML, CSS, Figma, Canva, Notion, Python* — whatever applies to that specific project

i. Add Another Divider

- Repeat the same step using “/divider” to maintain a clear structure.
j. Embed a Sample of the Project

Now it’s time to show proof of your work.

- Create a new heading, e.g., **“Project File or Image”**
  
To add a file (like a PDF):

- Click the **“+” icon**, then select **File**
- Upload your document directly, such as a written report or design mock-up

[Image by Author](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:1400/format:webp/1*8fmERd6pCBXnvA5gX99xrg.png)

To embed an image,
- Click the “+” icon, then scroll to find and select Image
- Upload a screenshot or graphic representation of the project
- You can also paste a public link to your project if it’s hosted online (e.g., Google Docs, GitHub, etc.)
  
k. Add a **“Result”** Section

- Add a final heading and title it, **“Results.”**
- Use this space to explain the result of the project briefly. For instance, ask yourself:
    - Did it solve a problem?
    - Was it well received?
    - Did it teach you something important?
  
l. Add a Cover Image to your project card

- Still within the new page, click on “*Add Cover.*”
- Go back to your project card
- Click on the settings icon
- Click on “*Layout.*”
- Click on “*Card Preview.*”
Lastly, click on “*Page Cover.*”

**STEP 6: Create the Contact Information Section**

Before we dive into the steps, it’s important to include **contact details** like your **Email Address**, **LinkedIn profile**, and **phone number** in your portfolio. It is also best to use clickable links for these — this way, potential employers can reach you easily without having to copy and paste details manually, thereby making the experience smoother and more professional.

a. Click on the **“+” icon**

b. In the dropdown search bar, type “column” and select the number of columns you want (e.g., 3) — Columns help to keep each contact information side-by-side.

[Image by Author](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:1400/format:webp/1*4IANh5lX6fLsWT5DCG8P4A.png)

c. In the **first column**,

- Type a label, e.g., **Email Me**
- Highlight the text, then in the box that appears, format the text however you want. For example:
  - Click on the “U” icon to underline
  - Click on the “A” icon to select your preferred font (text) color
  - Click on the **link icon**, then in the box that appears, type “mailto:” followed by your email address without any spaces ( e.g., mailto:johndoe@gmail.com)
  
d. In the **Second Column**,

- Type a label, e.g., **LinkedIn**
- Highlight the text, then in the floating toolbar:
- Click the **link icon**, then paste your LinkedIn profile URL (e.g., https://linkedin.com/in/yourprofile)
e. In the **Third Column**,

- Type a label, e.g., **Call Me**
- Highlight the text, then in the floating formatting toolbar:
- Click the **link icon**, then type, “tel:” followed by your phone number without any spaces (e.g., tel:+23480123456).
### How to Share Your Portfolio
Once your portfolio is ready, here’s how to make it accessible:

**a. Set the Page to “Public”**
Click on “Share” in the top-right corner of the page. Then toggle on “Share to web.” This makes your portfolio viewable by anyone with the link.

[Image by Author](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:1400/format:webp/1*lfRHYhRzLdPIOlgZ-zuQ5A.png)

**b. Customize Your URL**

Still in the Share menu, click “Copy link,” then choose “Settings” to customize the link. Use your name or something professional (e.g., notion.site/JohnPortfolio) so it’s easier to share and remember.

**c. Share It**

Include the link in your **job applications**, **social media bio**, **email signature**, or anywhere else people can discover your work.
